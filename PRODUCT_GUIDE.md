## The Power of Multi-Stage Workflows

Unlike chat-based AI that gives you one giant wall of text, ContentCraft breaks creation into purposeful stages. Each content type has its own specialized pipeline.

### Example: Creating an NPC
1. **Basic Info**: Name, appearance, background, class, and species
2. **Personality**: Traits, ideals, bonds, flaws, and motivations
3. **Stats**: Ability scores, saves, skills (auto-calculated with D&D 5e rules)
4. **Character Build**: Class features, racial traits, feats (automatically enriched)
5. **Combat**: Actions, reactions, attack modifiers
6. **Spellcasting**: Spell slots, spell list, save DC
7. **Legendary**: Legendary actions and lair effects
8. **Relationships**: Connections to other NPCs and factions
9. **Equipment**: Weapons, armor, magic items

**Each stage remembers the last.** Change the NPC's class in stage 1? The stats auto-update in stage 3.

### Example: Generating a Location
1. **Purpose**: What is this place? (tavern, dungeon, castle, hideout)
2. **Foundation**: Core concept, history, atmosphere
3. **Spaces**: Room-by-room generation with spatial relationships
4. **Details**: Descriptions, encounters, features
5. **Visual Map**: Auto-generated 2D grid map with rooms and doors
6. **Accuracy Refinement**: Door validation and spatial consistency checks

**See your dungeon take shape as you build it** with live map preview.

## Your Canon, Your Way

### The Canon Library
Think of it as your world's Wikipedia. Everything you generate gets stored as **entities**:
- **NPCs**: Characters with personalities, stats, and relationships
- **Locations**: Places with maps, descriptions, and encounters
- **Items**: Weapons, armor, magic items
- **Monsters**: Custom creatures with stat blocks
- **Spells**: Custom magic with mechanics
- **Factions**: Organizations and their members
- **Lore**: World history, legends, myths

### Scopes: Project vs. Library
- **Project Scope**: Content specific to one adventure or book
- **Library Scope**: Content shared across all your projects
- **Promote Content**: Move great NPCs from one adventure to your permanent library

### Collections
Group related entities together:
- "Core Party NPCs"
- "Sword Coast Locations"
- "Homebrew Monsters"
- "Magic Items - Uncommon"

Link entire collections to projects for instant access to all content.

### Fact-Checking & Consistency
- **Automatic Contradiction Detection**: "Wait, you said this character was an elf, but now they're human?"
- **Duplicate Claim Scanner**: Find where you've said the same thing twice
- **Canon Delta Resolution**: Review conflicts and choose which version to keep
- **Cross-Project Consistency**: Maintain the same facts when a character appears in multiple adventures

## Frequently Asked Questions

### "How is this different from ChatGPT?"
ChatGPT forgets. ContentCraft remembers. Every character, location, and detail is stored permanently in your Canon Library. ChatGPT gives you text. ContentCraft gives you structured, searchable, reusable game content.

### "Do I need to know how to code?"
Nope. ContentCraft is a web app. Point, click, create.

### "Can I use this for commercial projects?"
Yes. Everything you create in ContentCraft is yours.

### "What if I don't want to pay for API keys?"
Use **Manual Mode**. Copy ContentCraft's prompts to any free AI web interface (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini), paste the results back, and ContentCraft handles the organization and memory.

### "Does it work with [my favorite VTT]?"
ContentCraft exports stat blocks and maps in standard formats compatible with Roll20, Foundry, Fantasy Grounds, and other VTTs.

### "Can I import my existing content?"
Yes! Upload PDFs or paste text. ContentCraft will chunk and parse it into structured entities.

### "Is my content private?"
Yes. Your content is stored in your account and never shared. If you use Manual Mode, not even the AI provider sees your work (since you're copy-pasting locally).

### "What happens to my content if I cancel?"
You can export everything as JSON, Markdown, or plain text before canceling. Your content is always portable.

### "Can multiple people work on the same project?"
Not simultaneously in real-time, but multiple Sixsmith Games accounts can share exported content files.
